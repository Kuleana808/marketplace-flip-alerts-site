# FlipSignal — Initial Acquisition Map

## Founder objective
Get the first 3-5 engaged founder beta users from niches where:
- Marketplace behavior is already intense
- speed matters
- a better signal feed is obviously useful
- users already gather in communities we can enter manually

## Best first categories
1. appliances
2. furniture / couch flipping
3. phones / tech

## Category priority
### 1. Appliances
Why first:
- simple economics
- repeatable local demand
- less fraud complexity than phones
- clearer spread logic than mixed furniture

Best pitch:
- stop wasting time on junk washer/dryer listings
- get the appliance deals worth messaging first

## 2. Furniture / couch flipping
Why second:
- strong local behavior and community density
- very fast local sell/buy cycles
- highly visual filtering

Best pitch:
- get the couch and sectional flips worth checking before other buyers jump in

## 3. Phones / tech
Why third:
- speed matters a lot
- users feel the pain strongly
- but fraud/lock risk adds noise and trust issues

Best pitch:
- react faster to good local tech deals without refreshing all day

## Initial community / channel targets

### Appliances / general local flipping
- Reddit r/Flipping threads about sourcing and reseller Discords
- Reddit r/reselling discussions about sourcing communities
- SellerAider article listing reselling Discords, especially local flipping groups
- DISBOARD / Discadia search for reselling and flipping communities
- FlipFlip and similar reseller communities

### Furniture / couch flipping
- Facebook group: Couch Flipping Collective
- Facebook group: Couch Flipping Side Hustle
- Reddit: r/couch_flipping
- Reddit furniture-related flipping threads in r/Flipping
- furniture-flipper Facebook group roundups / blog posts

### Phones / tech
- Facebook group: Phone Flipping - eBay, OfferUp, Craigslist, iPhone/Samsung
- Facebook group: Phone Flipping Secrets & Strategies
- Reddit threads about flipping iPhones on Facebook Marketplace
- broader flipping / reselling Discords with active tech channels

## Outreach motion
1. join or observe the community
2. identify active posters or serious operators
3. start with direct conversational outreach, not a giant pitch
4. only send the page after interest
5. invite best-fit users into founder beta

## Suggested message angle by niche
### Appliances
"I'm testing FlipSignal, a sourcing product for local flippers. It helps surface appliance listings that actually look worth messaging first instead of wasting time on junk washer/dryer searches. Want the beta page?"

### Furniture
"I'm testing FlipSignal for couch/furniture flippers — basically a faster way to spot the local listings worth checking first instead of refreshing Marketplace all day. Want the founder beta page?"

### Phones / tech
"I'm testing FlipSignal for local phone/tech flips — focused on quicker deal signals and less search noise for iPhones/consoles/etc. Want the beta page?"

## Qualification questions
- what city/metro do you source in?
- what category do you know best?
- what minimum spread matters to you?
- how do you source today?
- do you want fewer better signals or more raw volume?

## Rule
Do not spam broad communities with links first.
Lead with a real conversation, then send the niche page.
