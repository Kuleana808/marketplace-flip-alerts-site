# Marketplace Flip Alerts — Live Research Notes (2026-07-05)

## What I checked
Live browsing and search-based research on:
- reseller cross-listing incumbents
- Facebook Marketplace deal-finder / alert tools
- reseller workflow pain surfaced in search results and public product pages

## Key findings

### 1. Cross-listing is crowded, which is why we should not start there
Live competitors found:
- List Perfectly
- Vendoo
- Crosslist
- ResellKit
- ListingJoy
- Sell The Flip

Observed pattern:
- the cross-listing category is mature
- incumbents already emphasize:
  - cross-posting to Facebook Marketplace
  - auto-delist / sales detection
  - analytics
  - AI listing help
  - inventory management

Evidence:
- List Perfectly homepage and pricing center the reseller workflow and Facebook Marketplace crosslisting
- Vendoo homepage says it is trusted by 81,000+ resellers and supports Facebook Marketplace among many channels
- both have multiple pricing tiers and broad feature bundles

Implication:
Do not start with a general cross-listing SaaS. It is a longer road, more crowded, and harder to wedge into fast.

### 2. There is a live category around flip alerts and Marketplace sourcing
Live competitors found:
- DealFlipAI
- Marketflip
- Flipify
- Marketplace Flipper
- SuperFlip AI content/comparison pages

Observed pattern:
- there are now dedicated tools for Facebook Marketplace deal discovery
- common promises:
  - alerts for new listings
  - margin/profit estimation
  - eBay price comparison
  - deal scoring
  - faster sourcing

Implication:
This is not a fake problem. A real category exists.

### 3. Current competitors still leave a wedge for us
Observed product positioning:
- DealFlipAI is very software-heavy and AI-score-heavy
- Marketflip emphasizes instant notifications across Facebook Marketplace, Kijiji, Craigslist, and OfferUp
- Flipify emphasizes active searches, eBay comparison, and a flip tracker

Gap we can attack:
- Many tools are still selling software first
- Our fastest path is to sell profit signal first
- concierge curation can beat generic noisy alert feeds for early users

Potential wedge:
- fewer alerts, higher quality
- narrower categories
- tighter metro-specific curation
- more practical decision framing
- human-reviewed or semi-reviewed deals for early pilot users

### 4. Search evidence suggests real pain around Marketplace search quality
Search result snippets surfaced repeated frustration themes:
- Facebook Marketplace search is terrible
- people build custom search combinations and hacks
- users want help finding deals faster
- sellers and buyers complain about spammy or useless inquiry patterns

Implication:
There is workflow pain before the transaction even starts.

## Competitor pricing snapshots

### List Perfectly
Pricing page observed:
- Simple: $29/mo
- Business: $49/mo
- Pro: $69/mo
- Pro Plus: $99+/mo

Takeaway:
Resellers do pay recurring subscriptions if the tool clearly saves time or grows sales.

### Vendoo
Pricing page observed:
- Starter: $12.49/mo billed yearly
- Growth: $24.99/mo billed yearly
- Pro: $49.99/mo billed yearly
- Enterprise: custom

Takeaway:
The reseller software market is comfortable with recurring spend roughly from low tens to low hundreds monthly.

### Flipify
Pricing page observed:
- Starter: $29/mo
- Flipper: $49/mo
- Pro: $99/mo

Takeaway:
Flip-sourcing software itself can command recurring pricing in the same range.

## CEO interpretation
This research supports the current strategy:
- do not start broad
- do not start with cross-listing
- do start with a lean sourcing edge tied directly to money

The category is real, but we should avoid competing head-on as a generic alert dashboard at first.

Best wedge now:
- concierge curation
- one or two categories
- one or two metros
- rapid feedback from paid pilot users

## Tactical product implication
Position the offer as:
- curated profitable Marketplace leads
- not another noisy scanner
- not another reseller OS
- not another all-in-one cross-listing platform
