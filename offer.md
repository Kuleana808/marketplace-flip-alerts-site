# Marketplace Flip Alerts — First Dollars Offer

## CEO decision
Under severe runway pressure, we are not starting with broad software.
We are starting with a concierge MVP that can get to first dollars fastest.

Chosen wedge:
- Facebook Marketplace sourcing arbitrage
- delivered as curated flip alerts for resellers
- later expanded into software once signal appears

Reason this wins right now:
1. It is directly tied to money.
2. Users already do this manually.
3. The value proposition is instant and obvious.
4. We can deliver a useful pilot before building a full product.
5. It can evolve into alerts software, comp scoring, and a seller dashboard later.

## Brand/trust rule
The GitHub Pages URL is a temporary staging surface, not the long-term buyer-facing brand.
For any serious outreach beyond close contacts or warm conversations, move this offer onto a branded custom domain or subdomain before expecting strong conversion.
A reseller is much more likely to trust:
- a clean branded domain
- a direct founder conversation
- a sample alert or live proof
than a raw github.io URL by itself.

## Product
Marketplace Flip Alerts

Promise:
Get hand-picked Facebook Marketplace deals with resale upside before you waste hours searching.

## Ideal pilot customer
- local flippers
- appliance/furniture resellers
- electronics flippers
- thrift/resale operators
- side-hustle resellers
- small dealer/buyer shops sourcing used inventory

## MVP delivery
For the pilot, deliver manually or semi-manually:
- daily or near-real-time curated deal alerts
- each alert includes:
  - item
  - asking price
  - estimated resale range
  - rough gross spread
  - quick rationale
  - source link
- delivered by Telegram, SMS, or email

## What customer buys
Not data.
Not scraping.
Not software.
They buy:
- more profitable opportunities
- less time wasted searching
- faster sourcing

## Founder-mode launch strategy
We should use a hybrid, not a pure paid-only or pure free approach.

Recommended sequence:
1. Run it ourselves first for a few days to generate real sample alerts and sharpen category judgment.
2. Give a very limited free founder beta to 3-5 qualified users only.
3. Convert the best free users into paid pilots fast.
4. Keep the standard paid offer visible so we do not anchor the market at zero.

Why:
- running it ourselves creates real proof and sample output
- a tiny free beta reduces trust friction
- unlimited free use attracts low-intent users and slows learning
- we still need to learn willingness to pay early

## Founding pilot pricing
Standard offer:
- $49 for 7-day trial pilot
- $149/month single category + one metro
- $299/month multiple categories or metros

Founder beta policy:
- free for 3-5 highly qualified users only
- must already flip actively in a clear category + metro
- beta lasts 5-7 days max
- goal is proof, testimonials, and conversion to paid pilot

Rule:
Do not run an open-ended free plan before we see conversion behavior.

## Positioning
Bad:
- AI flipping assistant
- marketplace intelligence platform
- sourcing OS

Good:
- We text you the best local flips worth pursuing.
- Stop refreshing Marketplace all day.
- Get curated resale opportunities with margin estimates.

## Success metric
The offer has signal if at least one of these happens quickly:
- users pay for a pilot
- users reply with category requests or geography requests
- users ask for faster alerts or more volume
- users forward alerts to partners/team members

## Pilot questions to learn PMF
Ask every prospect or pilot user:
1. What categories do you flip most?
2. What metro do you source in?
3. What minimum profit spread matters to you?
4. Do you care more about speed or accuracy?
5. Would you rather get fewer better alerts or more raw opportunities?
6. What do you use today — saved searches, hustle, VA, software, Discord, nothing?

## Next expansion if pilot works
1. Saved-search alert automation
2. Deal scoring model
3. Category templates
4. Telegram bot / dashboard
5. Multi-network sourcing beyond Facebook Marketplace
6. Browser extension or web app

## What we are NOT building yet
